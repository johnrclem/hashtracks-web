Design notes — Mock 01

  • · Leg gutter column (auto width) puts the LEG NN stamp in the left margin. Keeps it diegetic — it's the ticket sub-segment label, not a field label.
  • · Tilt retires at 2+ legs. Single-leg search keeps the -0.5deg prod tilt on hero variant; multi-leg stays axis-aligned so the stack reads as a cohesive ticket instead of a chaotic pile.
  • · Ghost row is a real <button> that unfolds into a full leg. Dashed-border + 40% opacity fill makes it read as pending, not interactive noise.
  • · "Where next?" hint answers the open question — yes, include it. Right-aligned, small caps, fades at the edge. No loud "+ Add Stop" label that would break the ticket metaphor.
  • · Continuity marks (three dots between legs) visually link consecutive rows without connecting them to the ghost slot (which should feel detachable).
  • · Start-date anchor surfaces in leg 02's sub-label: "anchored to Leg 01 end". When the user picks a date earlier than the anchor, inline error with a REQUIRED-style stamp fires.
  • · Submit row always shows aggregate window ("Apr 20 → Apr 26 · 6 nights") — the ticket's passenger-reading summary.

Design notes — Mock 02

  • · Overlap days get a paper-grain envelope. The whole Thursday block lives inside its own `travel-grain` card so the two LEG sub-bands read as separated sections of the same ticket, not two scattered ones.
  • · "◆ Transit day" stamp replaces the usual day title. Rotates -1.5deg, red monospace — same rubber-stamp vocabulary as the REQUIRED stamp on the search form.
  • · LEG sub-labels are `sequence · IATA` only, per the plan. No "Final day in LHR" / "Arrival day in CDG" copy — the IATA does the work.
  • · Perforation is the hairline variant (dashed-dotted 1px line with a centered ✈ glyph rotated -8deg in a white/ink-950 cut-through pill). It reads as an airline ticket's perforated tear-strip without being heavy. See Mock 06 for the dashed alternative.
  • · Distance-tier subheads survive inside each LEG sub-band (single LEG 02 section has both Nearby and Area tiers). Non-overlap days render with no LEG stamps — just the day header + tier subheads as today.
  • · REF codes extend from single-dest (REF-APR-23-XYZ) to `REF-THU-LHR→CDG` on overlap days. The ticket metaphor holds.

Design notes — Mock 03

  • · Each leg is its own boarding-pass panel with the LEG stamp in the top-left margin and a mini REF code top-right. Reads as three stacked tickets that belong to the same passenger.
  • · Stops as columns at ≥md breakpoint; stack vertically on mobile. Zero LEG sub-bands needed in this view because the leg IS the band.
  • · Compact card variant inside columns — "Confirmed · 18:30" collapsed into one line so three legs of Thursday+Friday events fit vertically without pagination.
  • · Panel footer: plain stat line (2 confirmed · 2 likely · 1 possible). Matches the existing SavedTripCard footer vocabulary.
  • · No perforation needed — overlap days reduce to the same date appearing in two columns. Visual delineation is column-level, not intra-day.

Design notes — Mock 04

  • · Route stamp uses the display font for the IATAs and ink-400 arrows as separators. Tabular-nums keeps three codes aligned.
  • · City subhead uses `·` as the separator, never `→` — the arrow is reserved for the route stamp's directional meaning.
  • · Stat line: `Apr 20–26 · 6 nights · 2 legs` — extends today's `Apr 20–23 · 3 nights` rhythm.
  • · Per-leg rows use the same compact LEG stamp from Mock 01, dimmed (ink-500) so they don't compete with the hero route stamp.
  • · Single-dest card stays almost unchanged vs today — just adds the "◆ ITINERARY" margin label + ".. · 1 leg" suffix on the stat line.
  • · Per-leg Remove/Edit intentionally NOT surfaced on the card — that's Phase 6.5 scope. The trip-level Remove archives the whole thing.

Design notes — Mock 05

  • · Multi-stop hero swaps the city h1 for the route stamp. Display font (Sora) + tabular-nums + soft ink-300 arrows. The REF-code and gate-stamp vocabulary land in the margin label.
  • · City subhead becomes the "read-out" of the codes. Spaces prevent the period separator from touching the letters — matches the boarding-pass `LONDON · PARIS · BERLIN` pattern.
  • · Radius in the stat line: for multi-stop, "50 / 50 / 30 km" (per-leg, aligned with column order). When all legs share a radius, collapse to "50 km" — pending product input.
  • · Single-dest hero unchanged — the existing code path at 1 leg doesn't render the ITINERARY margin label or route stamp.
  • · ROUTING REVISED / RADIUS ADJUSTED badges still fire above the hero on a per-leg basis when a fallback/snap happens. Suggest rendering them as compact per-leg inline hints under each leg's leg-row rather than the hero, because a hero-level badge can't disambiguate which leg expanded.
